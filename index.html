"Dr. Muhammad Yunus is a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur, banker, economist, and civil society leader. Born on June 28, 1940, in Chittagong, Bangladesh, he is best known for founding the Grameen Bank and pioneering the concepts of micro-credit and microfinance. These financial services provide small loans to the poor, particularly women, to help them start income-generating activities and alleviate poverty. Yunus received the Nobel Peace Prize in 2006 for his work in this field. He also holds several other prestigious awards, including the United States Presidential Medal of Freedom in 2009 and the Congressional Gold Medal in 2010. As of August 8, 2024, Dr. Yunus has been serving as the Chief Adviser of the Interim Government of Bangladesh"
